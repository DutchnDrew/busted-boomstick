About This Series

Why dragons? Because they're the perfect canvas for exploring extremes.

Every culture has its dragons — hoarders of treasure, keepers of wisdom, destroyers of worlds. When I paint them, I'm not interested in the fantasy cliché. I want to capture something primal. The red dragon isn't just fire; it's rage. The blue dragon isn't just cold; it's detachment. The emerald serpent isn't just ancient; it's the slow, inevitable weight of time.

These pieces came from the same place as my other work: the need to externalize what's inside. Fantasy gives us permission to paint the impossible, and in doing so, we reveal what's possible.
