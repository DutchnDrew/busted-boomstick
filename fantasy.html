Dragons on Vacation

Dragons on Vacation explores the playful contradiction between mythical power and mundane leisure. These ancient, fire-breathing beasts—so often trapped in tales of conquest and destruction—are finally taking a well-earned break. Whether they're sunbathing on the beach, sipping cocktails, or trying to squeeze into tourist T-shirts, these dragons embody a whimsical rebellion against their traditional narrative.

This piece reflects my love for blending fantasy with absurdity, creating worlds where the unexpected feels natural. Beneath the humor lies a gentle reminder: even the fiercest among us need rest. And maybe, just maybe, our inner dragons deserve a little sunshine.

About This Series

Why dragons? Because they're the perfect canvas for exploring extremes.

Every culture has its dragons — hoarders of treasure, keepers of wisdom, destroyers of worlds. When I paint them, I'm not interested in the fantasy cliché. I want to capture something primal. The red dragon isn't just fire; it's rage. The white dragon isn't just cold; it's isolation. Fantasy gives us permission to paint the impossible, and in doing so, we reveal what's possible.
