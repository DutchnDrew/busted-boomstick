Walking Between Worlds

An Art Book by Drew Eveland

"Art has always been a part of who I am—not as a distant ideal or career choice, but as a survival mechanism. As a combat veteran, I've seen firsthand what happens to the human mind and soul when placed in the crucible of war. Art became the bridge."

A collection of acrylic paintings exploring the dichotomy of color and shadow, the supernova and the silence. From underwater dreamscapes to explosive abstracts, this book chronicles one artist's journey through darkness toward light.

Inside the Book

Walking Between Worlds is organized into thematic sections, each exploring different emotional territories.

Beautiful Things

Explorations of love and joy — vibrant dragonflies, serene ocean horizons, and the gentle light that persists even after darkness.

Dark Things

Confronting the shadows — the weight of memory, the persistence of trauma, and the strange beauty found in difficult places.

The Process

An intimate look at how each piece begins as raw emotion — chaotic, urgent, cathartic — then slowly finds its form through refinement.

The Artist's Statement

A personal essay on art as survival, the journey from combat veteran to working artist, and finding humanity through creation.
