Charcoal Work

Grit, darkness, and the intimacy of hand-to-paper

I love the grittiness of charcoal, the deep blacks, the dirtiness involved in the actual creation process. Much of charcoal work involves REMOVING dark to lighten areas — it feels like carving, revealing a picture that's already there, not creating from nothing.

About Charcoal

Charcoal is raw. It's messy. It gets everywhere. And that's exactly why I love it.

Unlike other mediums where you add to create, charcoal often requires removing to reveal. You start dark and work light — carving away the darkness to find the image beneath. It feels less like painting and more like sculpting with shadow.

The deep blacks achievable with charcoal are unmatched. There's a richness, a velvety quality that other mediums can't replicate. And the texture — the grain of the paper, the grit of the charcoal — it all becomes part of the finished piece.
